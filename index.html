About

Short Biography

Dongxiao Zhu is a Professor in the Department of Computer Science at Wayne State University, where he received his Ph.D. from the University of Michigan, Ann Arbor. His research centers on Trustworthy AI and Applications — advancing adversarial robustness, explainability, and fairness of deep learning systems for use in health, urban, and social computing.

Dr. Zhu is the Founding Director of the Wayne AI Research Initiative, Inaugural Director of the Institute for AI and Data Science, and Director of the Trustworthy AI Lab at Wayne State University. He has authored 100+ peer-reviewed publications and serves on program committees of flagship venues including NeurIPS, ICML, ICLR, CVPR, AAAI, IJCAI, ACL, EMNLP, AMIA, and MICCAI. His research has been funded by NSF, NIH, and private agencies.

In addition to foundational AI research, Dr. Zhu pursues use-inspired AI research addressing pressing human-centered challenges — including mobility equity, health disparities, and future of work — with work featured in Detroit PBS, WJR Radio, and Hour Detroit Magazine. He is also deeply committed to AI education, having founded Wayne State's M.S. in AI program, now ranked #18 nationally.
